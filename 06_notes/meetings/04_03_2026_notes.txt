## Notes from Last Class Discussion

we were talking with Daniel about the feedback we received last friday and we are thinking on proposing some changes to the experimental setup. First, we are moving away from the idea of doing a 2-stage experiment becuase we realized (after Macartan pointed it out), that we don't really need that setup to test our hypothesis of belief updating after receiving performance information about the policy. Therefore, we are proposign a 2x2x3 setting (Punitive / Restorative justice polcy) x (Threat / Non Threat framing of targeted group) x (No information received / Positive Policy Performance / Negative Policy Performance)

this will create 12 different types of vignettes, but the magic of this type of conjoint experiments is that we are not trying to estimte 12 different coefficients, but collapse features to make precise comparisons. Another idea was to sacrifice a bit the number of questions and outcomes to explore, in order to gain more power by presenting more than one vignette to each respondent. For instance, one respondent can randomly be assigned to see 5 ou of the 12 possible scenarios, and we would ask the same questiosn on support for the policy after seeing each one. That will increase our analytical sample in multiple orders of magnitude
